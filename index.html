1.For a camel 👇

Few interesting facts about camel

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

2.For a chameleon 👇

Few interesting facts about chameleon

  1. THEIR FEET WORK LIKE SALAD TONGS .
  2. ALMOST HALF OF ALL KNOWN SPECIES LIVE IN MADAGASCAR .
  3. CHAMELEONS VARY WILDLY IN TERMS OF SIZE.

3.For a panda 👇

Few interesting facts about panda

  1. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  2. Pandas go from pink to white and black .
  3. A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.

4.For a teddy-bear 👇

Few interesting facts about teddy-bear

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.

5.For a pigeon 👇

Few interesting facts about pigeon

  1. Pigeons are highly sociable animal .
  2. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  3. Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.

5.For a flying-cicada 👇

Few interesting facts about flying-cicada

  1. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  2. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.
  3. Females may be attracted to the sound of motors. So you get to watch your dad, mom or older sibling get swarmed while they’re using the lawn mower or power tool.
